Farewell, But Not Goodbye

View the Project Slides:

Project Overview:

Saying goodbye is never easy, especially when it’s to colleagues who’ve become like family. As I bid farewell, I wanted this moment to reflect the vibrant memories we’ve shared and the bonds we’ve built.

The Inspiration Behind the Project

As part of my NLP class presentation, I embarked on a heartfelt project inspired by our WhatsApp group conversations. My goal was to create a digital reflection of our time together—capturing our interactions, shared experiences, and the unique dynamics of our group.

Unpacking the Data

The journey began with extracting and cleaning our WhatsApp data, a challenge tackled using Regex. From the refined dataset, I generated a word cloud that highlighted the most frequently used words. Unsurprisingly, words like 'class', 'thank you', and 'poll' stood out—reminders of our shared decision-making moments and collective humor. Our class decisions often revolved around polls, symbolizing our democratic spirit. Who could forget our amusing reluctance to appoint a class representative for most of the program?

Key Insights from the Data

Beyond word frequencies, I analyzed group contributions and was surprised to discover:
  • Most Active Member: Ghizlane Rehioui, despite spending just one year with us.
  • Top Contributors: Chaymae Bennouri (who later became our class representative) and myself.
  • Engagement Metrics: Total messages sent, media shared, and links posted.
Detailed insights, including visualizations and statistics, can be found in my presentation slides.

Unveiling Communication Patterns

Diving deeper, I mapped our activity spikes and identified key moments when our group came alive—like the fiery debate on whether an important exam should be scheduled in December 2023 or January 2024. That day, our group chat was nothing short of electric! Using NMF (Non-negative Matrix Factorization) for topic modeling, I uncovered recurring themes in our conversations, offering a richer understanding of our shared experiences and priorities.

A Heartfelt Farewell

To my UM6P School of Collective Intelligence Class of 2024: thank you for making these years so memorable. This analysis, shared with your permission, is a tribute to our shared journey. But this isn’t just a goodbye—it’s the start of a new chapter. I plan to dedicate weekends to sharing more ongoing and completed projects. So, let’s call this Week 1 of a new journey! Stay tuned, and thank you for being such an integral part of my story. With love and gratitude,
BABATUNDE, Ibukun

Approach & Methodologies:

  • Data extraction and cleaning using Regex.
  • Word frequency analysis to identify prominent terms.
  • Visualization with word clouds to capture conversational highlights.
  • Interaction analysis to map response patterns and activity peaks.
  • Topic modeling using Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) for thematic insights.

Key Findings:

  • Frequent words like 'class,' 'thank you,' and 'poll' showcased class dynamics.
  • Most active contributors were Ghizlane Rehioui, Chaymae Bennouri, and the author.
  • Analyzed group communication peaks during significant debates and events.
  • Uncovered key conversational themes through topic modeling.
  • Mapped detailed insights into interaction patterns and media sharing.

Technologies Used:

  • Regex
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  • Word Cloud
  • Topic Modeling
  • NMF
